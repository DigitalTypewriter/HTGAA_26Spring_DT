1. The stock concentration of a mystery substance (MS) is 5 M.
If the molar mass of MS is 532 g/mol, what’s the concentration of the stock concentration in g/mL? To make your life easier, you can use one of many online calculators.

**Answer: The stock concentration of MS is 2.66 g/mL.**<br>
**Steps:**<br>
> **1. Convert moles to grams. A 5 M solution contains 5 moles per liter: 5 mol/L * 532 g/mol = 2660 g/L**<br>
> **2. Convert grams per liter to grams per milliliter: 2660 g / 1000 mL = 2.66 g/mL**

2. You will perform a serial dilution to get 100 uM of MS. Devise a plan to dilute a 5 M MS solution to 100 uM. How many dilution steps will we need? Which tubes should we use? Which pipettes?

**Answer: Three dilution steps are needed using P10 & P1000 pipettes with three 1.5 mL microcentrifuge tubes.**<br>
**Steps:**<br>
> **1. Calculate the total dilution factor.** 5 M = 5 mol/L. The target is 100 µM = 1 × 10^-4 mol/L.  
> Total dilution factor = (5 mol/L) / (1 × 10^-4 mol/L) = 50,000.<br>
> **2. Break this into three steps.** 50,000 = 100 × 100 × 5, so we will do a 1:100 dilution, then another 1:100 dilution, then a 1:5 dilution.<br>
> **3. Use three 1.5 mL microcentrifuge tubes (Tube 1, Tube 2, Tube 3).**<br>
> • Tube 1: 1:100 dilution from the 5 M stock. Add 990 µL diluent + 10 µL of 5 M MS (P1000 + P10). Final concentration in Tube 1 = (5 M) / 100 = 0.05 M = 50 mM.<br>
> • Tube 2: 1:100 dilution from Tube 1. Add 990 µL diluent + 10 µL from Tube 1 (P1000 + P10). Final concentration in Tube 2 = (50 mM) / 100 = 0.5 mM = 500 µM.<br>
> • Tube 3: 1:5 dilution from Tube 2. Add 800 µL diluent + 200 µL from Tube 2 (both with P1000). Final concentration in Tube 3 = (500 µM) / 5 = 100 µM.<br>
